About

Where Heat Lingers is an interactive data visualization exploring urban heat inequality across New York City. Using public government temperature data, the project reveals how some boroughs and neighborhoods stay warmer than others.

CONCEPT

Heat does not affect every part of the city equally. Areas with fewer trees, more pavement, dense buildings, and limited green space often remain hotter for longer periods of time.

GOAL

Instead of showing data through a traditional chart, this project transforms the information into an atmospheric interactive map that feels visual, immersive, and easier to explore.

DATA SOURCE

Data comes from NYC Open Data's Hyperlocal Temperature Monitoring dataset, which tracks temperatures across boroughs to help study urban heat island conditions.

INTERACTIVITY

Users can compare hot and cold zones in the boroughs of NYC and compare them to the NYC redlining map. The map invites people to investigate where and why heat lingers most across New York City.

WHERE HEAT LINGERS

Public data can reveal invisible conditions in the city. This project uses design and interaction to make those patterns more visible.
